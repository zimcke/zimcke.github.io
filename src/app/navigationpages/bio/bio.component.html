How I grew up

I grew up on an isolated farm in Belgium that carries the name Zillekeshoeve. >>>>>>> 0625518d1721656d8a5b62619edea3b19d60488f My maternal grandparents moved there in 1949 (after the liberation). At that time there was no electricity nor running water for several years. The name stems from a Zil, an old, local unit for measuring farming land. One Zil measures the area of land that one farmer can plow by himself with horse and plough. In the case of my grandfather, that must have been quite a lot. In the 1980s my parents decided to stay on the farm, because they deemed living in solitude, but in the viscinity of family, a very rewarding experience.

My father is an experienced maker and DIYer. Needless to say, I grew up in a family that encouraged me to persue a career in STEM. In fact, as a 7-year old, my dad let me sort out his diodes by colour bands in his garage lab when I was bored...
