Ever since hearing my first song in Cantonese, I have become obsessed with Cantopop. I have spent hours listening to songs from different periods, in the beginning without comprehending one word of the lyrics. Even as a musicologist I was not aware this music existed and that, when looking at its popularity in Asia until today, it has the same energizing effect on me as on millons other people. Even though Cantopop became a recognized genre worldwide, there is still a lot of terrain that is unknown to non-Cantonese or non-Chinese speakers. Of course, being accessible to other nationalities was never its goal. But in a globalized world where English became the lingua franca it is actually very refreshing to listen to music that still guards its own principles.

In the past year I have been looking into scientific literature about Cantopop to get a better grip on its history. As one might expect, the number of English written sources is rather limited. However, there is one important exception: Hong Kong Cantopop - A Concise History by Yiu-Wai Chu, pubished by Hong Kong University Press. This book presents a history of Cantonese pop songs and links it to Hong Kong culture. I highly recommend it to anyone that wants to take a closer look at Cantopop. Unfortunately, it will probably not be easily available at you local library. That is why I assembled a playlist of the most important songs that are mentioned in the book. Because reading a music history book without taking the time to properly listen to the audio, does not really make sense. Below you will find a link to the playlist and the table of songs ordered per chapter of the book. Those who cannot obtain the book can still enjoy the music by itself.

Remark: the romanization below the characters is pinyin (Mandarin romanization), not jyutping (Cantonese romanization). This is due to limitations of the automatic romanization software used.
